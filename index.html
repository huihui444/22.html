Malaysia Youth Mental Health

Support, Awareness, and Resources

About the Project

This website aims to provide basic information and support resources for improving the mental health of teenagers in Malaysia. Our focus is on early screening, awareness, and connecting youths with professional help.

Why Mental Health Matters

Mental health is just as important as physical health. In Malaysia, mental health issues among youths have been rising. Early detection and support can prevent serious outcomes.

Key Support Areas

  • Regular school-based mental health screenings
  • Access to confidential counseling services
  • Peer support and safe spaces
  • Online and community mental health programs

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do I know if I need mental health support?

A: If you often feel sad, anxious, angry, or lose interest in things you used to enjoy, it might be good to talk to someone.

Q: Is it normal to feel stressed during school?

A: Yes, some stress is normal. But if it affects your sleep, appetite, or relationships, you should seek help.

Tips for Parents and Teachers

  • Encourage open communication and active listening
  • Recognize warning signs like social withdrawal or mood changes
  • Support children in seeking professional help if needed
  • Collaborate with school counselors or mental health units

Self-Check Tools

Here are some free tools to help identify mental health conditions:

  • DASS-21 Stress Test (mind.org.my)
  • PHQ-9 Depression Screener (MHA)

Policy and Legal Support in Malaysia

The Malaysian government has taken steps to improve mental healthcare, especially among students:

  • National Mental Health Policy (2012): Promotes early detection, community awareness, and mental wellness.
  • Mental Health Act 2001: Provides legal protection for mental healthcare delivery and patient rights.
  • School Mental Health Program (2017): Supports screenings, peer support groups, and referrals in public schools.
